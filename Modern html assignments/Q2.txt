HTML entities are special codes used to represent reserved characters or characters with a specific meaning in HTML. They are often used to ensure proper rendering of characters that might otherwise be interpreted as HTML markup. Five commonly used HTML entities:

&lt; - Less Than Sign: Represents the less than symbol (<).

&gt; - Greater Than Sign: Represents the greater than symbol (>).

&amp; - Ampersand: Represents the ampersand symbol (&).

&quot; - Double Quotation Mark: Represents the double quotation mark (").
